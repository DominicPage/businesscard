What it's all about

I thought it might be nice for my existing, or any potential new customers to get to know me a little better. Maybe you're in two minds to book, or you just curious after already seeing me several times before. Either way I thought this would be a cute little way of providing you all with some extra information about me, my salon and experience... and a few little bonus items!

My Experience

Lithuania is renown for its beauty industry and the education and standard there sits near the very top of what Europe has to offer.

My beauty education strted at school and university and it wasn't long before my artistic styles got me recognised. I was working for one of the top Salon's in Vilnius (the capital city of Lithuania) where i learned from some truly amazing people. After moving to Guernsey I kept up my skills by working on my friends nails for free, but as word of mouth spread and I started hearing about the feedback on the work I was doing I decided to look for opportunities to get back into the industry.

My Journey to this point

Originally from Lithuania, I moved to London in June 2009, this being my first real attempt at life out of my own country, not speaking a word of the local language it was a massive culture shock. However, even with the initial language barrier it did not take me long to realise that I was not a city girl. Looking at what options may have been available to me I was told about this little gem in the English Channel named Guernsey! A quick conversation with a friend who lived there convinced me to immediately pack my things and continue my journey onwards to what was almost a completely unknown destination.

It did not take me long to realise one of the first drawbacks to living on Guernsey... Trying to explain to people where it actually is! It took a very long and detailed conversation with my parents to narrow it down to this tiny rock off the coast of France. There initial question of "But... why?!" did seem to make quite a lot of sense at the time, and when it was asked I did not initially have an anwer for them, but it didn't take me long to discover the natural beauty of the island and after a few images of the cobo sunset were sent home I think my family quickly realised the answer!

I spent most of my time here just getting used to the language. Still being a relatively new English speaker it was a big challenge for me to adapt to working life especially and it took me about 7 years of living on the island to get enough confidence to get back into beauty. Always practicing on with my friends coming up with new design I was always a very artistic person and this work was a real passion for me so I was so happy to get back into it.

It didn't take me long to realise that people were really appreciating the work I was doing. I decided to get my head down, work hard at build up enough money to move into my own place. The journey for me has been amazing, thinking where I was less than 10 years ago to where I am now. Hopefully this is just the start of a new beginning with plenty more of the journey left to go...
